Thomas D. Brown

I am a PhD student in the Philosophy department at the University of Liverpool.

My primary research interests are metaphysics and the philosophy of language. For my PhD project, I aim to develop what I call an asymmetric theory of propositions. Such a theory will properly account for the ontological asymmetry between true and false propositions.

 

publications

'Propositions are not representational', Synthese (forthcoming).

I argue against the prevalent view that propositions are representational entities. In the final section of the paper, I also advance the claim that true and false propositions are ontologically asymmetric.
